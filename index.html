ABOUT DANIEL

Daniel has been working at the interface between data science, information technology, and research for 25 years. After completing his education with a focus on computer science, he studied economics at the University of Innsbruck and earned a doctorate in innovation and strategy. In addition to numerous academic articles, he also publishes in practice-oriented magazines.

In practical market research, his main expertise lies in quantitative research. His technical background allows him to use complex statistical methods to generate facts and insights from numbers. Rather than individual opinions, he focuses on large groups of participants in his studies. He has his own AI data analysis platform with dashboards to handle large studies.

In information technology, Daniel has strong expertise in domain-specific language (DSL) design, with a deep understanding of how to tailor language constructs to efficiently model, simplify, and automate tasks within specialized domains. In parallel, Daniel possesses advanced skills in integrating large language models (LLMs) into domain-specific workflows, leveraging their generative and interpretative capabilities to enhance user interaction, automate reasoning, and drive intelligent decision-making. His work bridges the gap between human intuition and machine precision, making complex systems more accessible and intelligent through thoughtfully designed language and AI integration.
